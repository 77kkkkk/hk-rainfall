Rainfall in Hong Kong

Seasonal Rainfall in Hong Kong - 2020

The rainfall trend for 2020 shows noticeable seasonal variation, with the highest levels occurring in the summer months. Winter (blue) remains relatively dry, with only a few instances of minor rainfall. Spring (green) marks an increase in precipitation, with more frequent spikes, peaking just before summer. Summer (pink) exhibits the most intense rainfall events, with several extreme spikes exceeding 100mm, indicating possible typhoon or monsoon effects. Autumn (purple) also records significant rainfall, with one extreme event surpassing 175mm. This suggests that both summer and autumn were the wettest seasons in 2020, while winter remained the driest.

Seasonal Rainfall in Hong Kong - 2021

The rainfall trend in 2021 follows a similar seasonal pattern to 2020, but with more extreme fluctuations. Winter (blue) again experiences minimal rainfall. Spring (green) shows slight increases, though still relatively low compared to later seasons. Summer (pink) brings several notable rainfall peaks, with multiple instances exceeding 50mm. However, the most dramatic rainfall occurs in autumn (purple), where a major peak surpasses 300mm, indicating a heavy storm or typhoon event. The overall trend suggests that while summer was rainy, autumn saw the most extreme downpours.

Seasonal Rainfall in Hong Kong - 2022

The 2022 rainfall trend is characterised by a more balanced distribution across the seasons, though summer and autumn remain dominant. Winter (blue) again records minimal rainfall, while spring (green) shows a gradual increase, with occasional peaks surpassing 50mm. Summer (pink) experiences multiple intense rainfall events, including one extreme spike exceeding 150mm. Autumn (purple) continues to bring substantial rainfall, with notable peaks but slightly lower intensity than in 2021. This data suggests that while summer was still the wettest season, autumn saw moderate but consistent heavy rainfall.

Seasonal Rainfall in Hong Kong - 2023

The rainfall trend for 2023 exhibits a distinct shift, with autumn becoming the most extreme season. Winter (blue) remains mostly dry, while spring (green) has a few moderate spikes, similar to previous years. Summer (pink) experiences several heavy rainfall events but does not reach the same intensity as autumn. The most significant feature of this graph is the extreme rainfall in autumn (purple), where multiple peaks exceed 400mm. This suggests that 2023 experienced one of the most intense autumn rainfall events in recent years, possibly due to typhoons or unusually strong monsoon activity.

Seasonal Rainfall in Hong Kong - 2024

The 2024 rainfall data presents an interesting trend, with a clear distinction in seasonal rainfall distribution. Winter once again records minimal precipitation, maintaining its trend as the driest season. Spring sees a gradual increase, with rainfall events becoming more frequent and intense by late May. Summer records significant rainfall, with multiple peaks exceeding 80mm, indicating potential typhoon activity. However, autumn stands out as the most intense season, with numerous extreme spikes reaching over 100mm. This suggests that autumn continues to be a dominant season for rainfall in Hong Kong, possibly driven by stronger storm systems or shifting weather patterns.
